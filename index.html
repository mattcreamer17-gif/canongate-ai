§ 2. How we would think about your stack

Three layers. One discipline: know which layer each problem belongs on.

60%

Ordinary code and database work

Ingestion, de-duplication, jurisdiction tagging, versioning, audit logs, review queues. Postgres, jobs, schemas. The boring half that decides whether the pretty half can be trusted.

30%

Rule-based logic

Peril taxonomy, scoring thresholds, methodology rules an underwriter can read, sign, and defend. Deterministic where determinism is a feature, not a bug.

10%

Genuine LLM work

Synthesis, classification into perils, drafting the reasoning trail a human can sign off. Narrow, evaluated, auditable. Not where the product lives. Where the product gets its voice.

Teams who blur those layers waste model spend putting language models where Postgres would be faster, cheaper, and auditable. Teams who get the split right ship faster and survive the first eval disagreement with a tier-one carrier.

§ 3. The hypothesis we would test on a call

Three statements. Thirty seconds of your time each.

  1. H1

    Evaluation and audit-trail burden

    Right now, when an underwriter disputes a score moving two points in a week, the audit trail lives in Tim's head and a notebook. That does not clear the next two pilots.

  2. H2

    Context bleed across perils and jurisdictions

    A Venezuela oil-nationalisation signal should not creep into Brazil SRCC scoring. The natural failure mode of a growing corpus is exactly that. The fix is structural, not prompt-level.

  3. H3

    US GTM stretch

    An Edinburgh team of 2 to 10 running a Miami booth means every hour at the booth is an hour not on the pipeline. Who services the US leads you generate this week, and on what timeline, before they stale.

§ 5. The paper to read before the call

Interpretable Context Methodology (ICM).

Submitted to ACM TiiS. Repo is MIT-licensed. The argument: agent context can be organised as a layered filesystem (L0 identity through L4 working artifacts), with measurable interpretability and reproducibility gains. It was written for your problem shape: bounded-but-large corpus, multiple jurisdictions and perils, agents that need to know what they know and where each claim came from.

github.com/RinDig/Interpretable-Context-Methodology-ICM-

One related thread worth a glance, given your audience of specialty insurers and conservative governance readers: the Ethics Engine, a psychometric assessment tool for evaluating ideological and moral patterns in LLMs. arxiv.org/abs/2510.11742 and github.com/RinDig/AuditEngine.
